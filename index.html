COST SPLIT CALCULATOR

Fairly calculate the cost of an expense based on each person's income or budget.

This calculator can be used to fairly split a bill in proportion to each participant's income. This is perfect for partners where each person has a different budget or income and so splitting the bill in the typical 50/50 fashion isn't really fair. Here the expense is the same percentage of the participant's budget.

Populate the expense column with the total amount that you are trying to split up. This column is defaulted to 510

For each participant, populate their respective row with their name and budget. The current defaults are "Melany 130000" and "Bastien 120000". Should you need more rows, use the "Add a Participant" button on the button right.

Once all of the needed information has been provided, use the "Calculate" button. This will run the calculations and populate the result table below and the written explanation under the results table.

The default for this calculator is 2 participants, but more participants can be added using the "Add a Participant" button at the bottom left of the calculator.

To remove participants, use the "Remove" button to the right of the calculator.

After pressing the "calculate" button under the calculator, a table will fill out with all of the information for easy view, and below the table you will find a verbal explanation.

"Expense contribution" and "% of Expense" signify the proportion of the total expense that participant is paying, whereas "% of income/budget" is used to show how much of that participant's budget will be allocated to the expense.

Bastien's yearly income is $120000 and his partner, Melany, earns a yearly income of $130000. Their rent is $510 each month. Using this calculator, we find that if Melany pays $262.20 per month, and Bastien pays $244.80, they are each equally proportioning 0.20% of their income towards the rent. Yes, Melany is paying a little more each month, but it is proportionally acceptable since her budget is bigger.
